## 2. Maker's first comment (the heart of the launch)

> Post this the moment you go live. It's first-person, story-first — the format PH rewards.

```
Hey Product Hunt 👋 I'm John, the maker of MukGo.

MukGo didn't start with a big idea. It started with frustration.

I've driven hundreds of thousands of miles across America — LA to Dallas more times
than I can count, RV trips, endless business drives. And every trip, I kept making
the same four mistakes:

🚻 "Why did I stop here AGAIN?" — a restroom so dirty I'd sworn never to return.
   Months later, I was back.
⛽ "I'm almost out of gas" — and my nav kept pointing me to a station 10 miles
   BEHIND me.
🍜 "I just want Vietnamese food" — Google gave me Chinese, Italian, pizza,
   everything except a decision.
📍 "Where is everyone?" — every meetup ended in ten text messages.

None of these are catastrophes. They're small. But they repeat until you just accept
them as normal. One day I stopped accepting them.

So I built MukGo — not another map or restaurant finder, but a decision engine for
drivers. The idea is simple: don't show me everything. Show me the few things worth
stopping for, ahead of me, right now.

What makes it different:
• Ahead-only — food & gas in your direction of travel, never behind you
• Verified-clean — drivers rate bathroom & food-area cleanliness (this exists on
  NO other map app — and honestly it's the thing people react to most)
• 5 choices, one glance — big cards built for driving, not doom-scrolling
• Meetup Mode — see everyone's live ETA instead of texting "where are you?"

It's live in Google Play open testing, in 5 languages, on real data. Gas search is
always free and unlimited, because running low on fuel is a safety issue.

I'm here all day. Tell me where it breaks, what's missing, what you'd never use.
That feedback is exactly what I need right now. 🙏

— John
```

---

### Notes
- **Don't attack Google.** PH audiences dislike it and it's off-brand — you win on *decisions*, not by dunking on search.
- **The restroom angle is your press hook.** Journalists and PH commenters both latch onto "the app that rates restrooms." Lead visuals with it.
- **Authenticity > polish.** The founder story is your unfair advantage here. Keep the maker comment human and specific.

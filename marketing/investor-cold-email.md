# MukGo — Investor Outreach Email Kit

*Goal: one thing only — get the meeting. Not to explain everything.*
*Stage framing: pre-seed/seed; product live in Google Play open testing; raising to reach
public launch + data density. Tone: founder-direct, zero hype (per brand voice).*

**Rules of the game**
- Under 150 words for the first touch. Investors read on phones, between meetings.
- Lead with the founder insight or traction — never "I hope this finds you well."
- One CTA: a specific, low-friction ask ("20 minutes next week?").
- Personalize the first line per investor (the `[⚡]` slot) — one researched sentence
  beats ten generic paragraphs.
- Never attach the full deck cold; link or offer it. Attachments trip spam filters.

---

## 1. Subject line bank (A/B rotate)

1. `The map apps forgot the driver` *(insight angle — recommended default)*
2. `Rating America's restrooms (seriously) — MukGo` *(pattern-interrupt, press-proven hook)*
3. `Live on Google Play: a decision engine for drivers` *(traction angle)*
4. `240M drivers, zero apps built for the stop` *(market angle)*
5. `[Name] — 100,000 miles led to this` *(founder angle, personalized)*

Avoid: "Investment opportunity," "Quick question," anything with 🚀 or ALL CAPS.

## 6. Personalization slots (the `[⚡]` line)

Research 2 minutes per investor; pick one:
- **Thesis match:** "You wrote that [thesis] — that's exactly our bet with [X]."
- **Portfolio echo:** "You backed [company]; we're doing for road stops what they did for [category]."
- **Stage/geo fit:** "You lead pre-seed consumer rounds in [region] — that's us, precisely."
- **Personal:** "Heard you on [podcast] on proprietary-data moats — that's our whole model."

If you can't fill the slot honestly, that investor isn't researched enough to email yet.

| They say | You answer |

| "Can't Google build this?" | "They can copy a feature in a week. They can't copy years of driver-generated cleanliness data and personal decision history — the moat is time + trust, not code." |

## 8. Sending discipline
- **Batch small:** 10–15 investors per week, so every `[⚡]` line is real.
- **Sequence:** Day 0 opener → Day 4–5 follow-up → Day 10–12 breakup. Stop after three.
- **Track:** a simple sheet — name / fund / thesis fit / date sent / reply / next step.
- **Never:** mass-BCC, "Dear Investor," fake urgency, or a deck attachment on touch one.
- **Signature block:** name · MukGo · one-line descriptor ("a decision engine for
  drivers") · Play Store link. No logos, no quotes, no calendly-wall (offer times in-line
  first; Calendly link is fine in follow-ups).

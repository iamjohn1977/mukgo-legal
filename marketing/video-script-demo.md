# MukGo — Short-Form Demo Video Scripts

*"Same drive, with MukGo." For TikTok / Reels / YouTube Shorts. Vertical 9:16.*
*Based on the MukGo Promotion & Messaging Playbook. Promise: "Stop with confidence."*

**Golden rules for short-form**
- The first 3 seconds decide everything. Lead with the frustration, not the logo.
- Show the phone screen doing the thing — real UI, real driving POV.
- On-screen captions always (80% watch muted). VO optional.
- One idea per video. End on the same CTA every time.

---

## HOOK BANK (A/B test these first lines)

Test 3–5 as separate uploads; double down on whichever holds retention past 3 sec.

1. "You already passed the gas station your app is recommending." *(pain, oddly specific)*
2. "I'm not opening that restroom door again." *(visceral)*
3. "300 restaurants. I wanted ONE." *(relatable overwhelm)*
4. "Every map shows you everything. Here's why that's the problem." *(reframe)*
5. "The one thing no map app will tell you about your next stop." *(curiosity)*

## PRODUCTION NOTES
- **Music:** driving/lo-fi beat with a clear "drop" at the MukGo reveal (0:05 in A, 0:15 in B).
  The audio flip from tense → resolved sells the "different ending."
- **Screen capture:** record real in-app footage at 60fps; zoom slightly on the tap moments.
- **Captions:** big, high-contrast, bottom-third-safe (above the platform UI). Match the
  neon brand — cyan/magenta text on dark.
- **Faceless-friendly:** both scripts work with zero on-camera presence (POV + screen only),
  which is ideal for volume testing. A founder-face version of Script B (John talking to
  camera between shots) tends to build more trust — test both.
- **Series idea:** turn the hook bank into an ongoing series — one frustration per video,
  each resolving in MukGo. Cheap to produce, compounds reach.
- **Do NOT** name-and-shame competitors on screen (say "your map app," not "Google"). Keeps
  it brand-safe and avoids the off-brand dunk.
